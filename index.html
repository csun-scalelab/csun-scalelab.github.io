California State University, Northridge — Mechanical Engineering

Advancing the science of bio-inspired motion

SCALE Lab combines physics-based models with data-driven approaches to study and control bio-inspired dynamical systems — from fish schooling to AI-powered swimmers and decentralized multi-agent systems.

Research Focus

◈

Fish Locomotion & Schooling

Studying fluid-structure interactions and how fish leverage hydrodynamic benefits in large groups using a hierarchy of interaction models.

△

AI-Powered Swimmers

Using Reinforcement Learning to train optimal locomotion controllers and discover effective mechano-sensory cues for bio-inspired robotic swimmers.

◯

Decentralized Multi-Agent Control

Developing mathematical models for decentralized biological systems — from sea stars and tube feet — to design autonomous distributed robotic systems.

◆

Fluid-Structure Interaction

Building physics-based models combined with data-driven approaches to study how biological systems interact with and exploit their fluid environments.
